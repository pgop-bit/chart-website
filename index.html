The Problem

Existing frameworks
overlook climate resilience.

Offshore wind potential remains untapped in the United States, where only 42% of offshore areas with >7 m/s wind speeds have been leased for development. These turbines operate for 25-30 years, but existing siting frameworks rely on static historical data, missing both climate resilience considerations and the full scope of environmental and socio-technical constraints.

CHaRT utilizes comprehensive siting criteria paired with CMIP6 mid-century projections directly into the siting index, surfacing sites that remain viable through the full operational lifetime of the infrastructure.

Model Validation

The numbers behind CHaRT.

R² = 0.91 Neural network accuracy across SSP5-8.5 climate scenario
+2.11 pts BOEM lease area advantage over non-lease offshore waters
138,625 km² High-resilience zones identified — 14× the DOE 30 GW target area
p < 0.001 Statistical significance of lease area validation

CHaRT's Weighted Linear Combination (WLC) suitability index was validated against 64,611 grid cells across 1.6 million km² of U.S. continental offshore waters. Existing BOEM lease areas, which constitute expert regulatory lease placements in place for current or future turbine development, scored higher than non-lease waters across all three climate scenarios, reinforcing the model's validation against known viable sites.

For climate resilience, a feedforward neural network utilizing TensorFlow/Keras with three hidden layers was trained to predict suitability from the eight standardized criteria. The neural network achieved R² values of 0.88–0.91, confirming that the WLC framework correctly captures the relationships between siting criteria across scenarios.
